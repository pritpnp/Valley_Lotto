A Forward-Thinking Retailer Sets the Standard for Lottery Sales in District 4A
Brad Gordner opened @YourConvenience in South Williamsport 
in Lycoming County nearly a decade ago on the strength of a 
single conviction: he saw an opportunity and he took it. This 
August 8 marks the store’s 10th anniversary, and the results 
speak for themselves.  

@YourConvenience has grown into one of the highest-volume 
Lottery retailers in District 4A — and by most accounts, the 
highest — drawing customers from across the region to a store 
that has made itself synonymous with winning.

A Destination Store

@YourConvenience serves a loyal local base, but its reputation 
extends well beyond the neighborhood. Customers travel from 
Lock Haven, Lewisburg and other communities specifically to 
play their numbers there. The store has sold a $1 million Scratch-
Off winner and a $500,000 Cash 5 with Quick Cash jackpot-prize. 
Gordner makes sure those moments stay visible — winning check 
photos line the walls, and P.O.S. materials are kept current and 
prominent throughout the store. “People see those big winners 
and ask if we’ve had any lately,” Gordner said. “We can point right 
to them. It makes it a lot easier to recommend an extra ticket.”

A cornerstone of that reputation is his payout policy: @
YourConvenience pays winning tickets up to $2,500 regardless of 
where they were originally purchased. “If we pay you, you’re going 
to buy here,” Gordner explained. “And you’re going to come back.”  
The store is open:

•  Weekdays 6 a.m. - midnight 
•  Saturdays 7 a.m. - midnight 
•  Sundays 8 a.m. - midnight

These hours of operation give customers consistent access and 
plenty of reasons to make that destination their go-to-stop.

The Daily Second-Chance Drawing and the Lottery Signature Book

Gordner has run one of his most popular promotions for nearly 
the entire life of the business: a daily Second-Chance Drawing. 
It works like this. Customers drop their non-winning tickets into 
a barrel at the counter, and each day one ticket is drawn — the 
holder wins $10. It costs the store relatively little, but the return 
on repeat visits and customer loyalty is significant. “Everybody 
knows it,” Gordner said. “It’s always full. It’s just a given.”
More recently he introduced a companion initiative: a Lottery 
Signature Book. Customers who purchase Lottery can sign the 
book once per day during the week. At the end of each week, 

all seven daily $10 drawings are announced together, giving 
customers a cumulative chance at $120 in prizes simply by visiting 
regularly. It draws on a traditional sign-in book familiar from social 
clubs and VFW halls, but applied to a Lottery retail environment 
in a way that is genuinely novel. As the book’s participant pool 
grows, Gordner envisions an annual grand prize drawing open to 
everyone who has signed it throughout the year — another layer 
of engagement that rewards consistency and keeps customers 
coming back.

Taking Lottery to YouTube

Gordner is also investing in a YouTube channel called 
“ScratchinLotto TV”, where he posts videos of ticket scratching 
and store activity to build an audience beyond the immediate 
community. The channel is still in its early stages, but his vision is 
clear: a growing subscriber base that translates into foot traffic, 

brand loyalty, and new customers who discover the store online 
and make the trip in person. To kick off growth, he is offering 
a $500 a Week for Life Lottery ticket to each of the first 1,000 
in-store subscribers. “The goal is to market it broadly but keep it 
localized,” he said. “Get people to interact with it by coming in.”

A Partnership Built on Trust

Gordner credits his relationship with District Sales Representative 
Joshua Mont as an important part of the store’s success. “The 
Lottery team has always been very, very good to us,” he said. 
Mont returned the compliment: “People often talk about out-
of-the-box thinking as a key to success. With Brad, there really is 

no box. He does things that are so unique and different — and 
you can see that just by being here.” Gordner’s advice to other 
retailers on that relationship is simple: communicate openly, stay 
informed, and make sure every staff member knows the product 
well enough to deliver a seamless experience for every customer.

Advice for Fellow Retailers

Gordner’s philosophy comes down to three qualities: patience, 
persistence, and reinvention. “Patience when things go wrong. 
Persistence to keep going. And reinvention to stay fresh,” he 
said. “You can’t just stay the same. You have to keep reinventing.” 

As @YourConvenience approaches its 10th anniversary, that 
restless creativity — from a barrel full of non-winning tickets to a 
growing digital presence — is exactly what has kept it at the top 
of District 4A.

SIDEBAR: The “Second-Chance” Barrel - A simple promotion with lasting impact
The daily Second-Chance Drawing at @YourConvenience is 
one of the most replicable promotions in this issue — low cost, 
easy to manage, and proven over nearly a decade. Customers 
bring their non-winning Scratch-Offs and Draw Game tickets to 
the store and drop them in a barrel at the counter. Once a day, 
one ticket is drawn and that customer wins $10. Winners are 
announced on the store’s Facebook page.
The cost is modest — $70 per week — but the return is real: 
customers who might otherwise discard their non-winners 

have a reason to come back. Repeat visits drive new purchases, 
and the barrel itself serves as a constant visual reminder of the 
promotion. Gordner has paired it with a Lottery Signature Book, 
letting customers sign in once daily for an additional weekly 
drawing, compounding the incentive to visit regularly.
For any retailer looking for a straightforward way to reward 
regulars and increase visit frequency, the second chance barrel 
is a proven starting point
