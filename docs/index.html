Win odds (1:X) is the number that matters. It's the chance a ticket wins any prize — and since the cheap break-even prizes hugely outnumber the jackpots, it's effectively a low-prize-weighted figure (lower = better chance to win something). 🔻 WEAK ODDS = worse than 1:4.5, a poor game to stock. Prizes left (est.) = how much of the whole game is unsold (estimated from the top prizes — works because prizes are shuffled evenly through the pack, so it tracks the cheap prizes too). 🟠 SWAP = under 40% left. Lower prizes left = non-jackpot wins still in the pack. 🔴 ENDED = sales stopped.
A separate single-day "low-prize %" can't be computed — on any one day it's mathematically the same as the top-prize %, so we don't fake one.
